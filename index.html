Interactive Wearable Art Piece

I worked on the electronics, CAD, and served as a 3D printing expert on a team of 5 students as we worked to make something meaningful in the realm of wearable science fiction. Our website is here and a video of our wearable being worn can be found here.

3D Scanner

A partner and I designed, built, and wrote firmware for a fully functional 360 degree 3D scanner. It runs off of an Arduino Uno, an infrared distance sensor, and two servos. The data it collects is then passed through serial to a computer and visualized in 3D. See a video of it working here.

Jimmy, the Humanoid Robot

I spent a semester at Olin researching and developing more seamless modes of interaction beteween humans and robots. During this semester of research I wrote a function using python and ROS that allowed Jimmy to move his head to follow your face when you spoke to him. Check out my code here.

Web Enabled Lighting

My roommate and I strung multi-color LED lights around the perimeter of our dorm room and enabled them to be controlled from a website. Here's a link to our git repo.

Patent Filing

I filed my own utility patent for an apparatus that prevents moisture absorbtion into 3D printing filaments during use and long-term storage. My apparatus is currently patent pending.

Minimalist Approach to Human Robot Interaction

A teammate and I have been exploring the area of human-robot interaction with the goal of creating a relatively minimal robot that can convey emotion. We are now on our second robot implementation.

Analog Control of a DC Motor

A teammate and I implemented position control for a DC motor without any added sensors. Here is our writeup detailing the circuit and the math behind it all.

Computer Vision Drawing Application

A teammate and I built a webcam-based drawing application using a computer vision library called openCV. You can choose your drawing color by picking up any colorful object around you. Check out our code.
